Central Turkey

Central Turkey includes sites east of Istanbul and Pamukkale, extending to an eastern boundary running from the Black Sea coast through Sivas down to Antakya (Antioch on the Orontes).

The region encompasses Paul's first missionary journey through South Central Anatolia, the Hittite heartland in the north, and the rugged Mediterranean coast.

North Central Turkey

North Central Anatolia includes Ankara (Turkey's capital and home of the Museum of Anatolian Civilizations), the Hittite capital Hattusha/Hattusas, Yazilikaya, Aizanoi, and several Black Sea coastal cities including Sinope and Amisus (Samsun).

South Central Turkey

South Central Turkey traces Paul's first missionary journey through Derbe, Lystra, Iconium (Konya), and Pisidian Antioch. The region also includes the Hittite site of Eflatunpinar, and the remarkable hilltop city of Sagalassos.

Mediterranean Coast

The Mediterranean coast of Central Turkey includes Perge, Aspendos, Side, Tarsus (birthplace of the Apostle Paul), the Cilician Gates, and the harbor city of Antalya — all significant in both the Roman world and Paul's missionary journeys.
