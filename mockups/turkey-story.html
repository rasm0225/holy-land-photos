Turkey

Turkey spans one of the world's great crossroads of civilization — from the Bosphorus to the Euphrates, from the Black Sea to the Mediterranean. Its archaeological landscape includes sites central to the Old and New Testaments, the Seven Churches of Revelation, and the earliest known human structures on earth.

Western Turkey

Western Turkey covers sites from Istanbul and the Bosphorus south to Pamukkale, including the seven churches of Revelation. Major sites include Ephesus, Laodicea, Miletus, Pergamum, Sardis, Smyrna/Izmir, and Hierapolis.

Central Turkey

Central Turkey stretches from the Anatolian plateau to the Mediterranean coast, encompassing Paul's first missionary journey cities — Derbe, Lystra, Iconium, and Pisidian Antioch — as well as Hittite capitals, ancient harbors, and the cities of Perge, Aspendos, and Tarsus.

Eastern Turkey

Eastern Turkey contains some of humanity's oldest sites — including Göbekli Tepe (ca. 9,600 B.C.), the oldest known monumental architecture on earth. The region also includes Haran, the Euphrates River valley, Nemrut Dagi, and significant Syro-Hittite sites.
